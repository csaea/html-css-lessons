Welcome to the Year 1999

When HTML and CSS ruled the interwebs.

It is the year 1999. The internet is a thing. Email is a thing. But memes are not a thing. Social media is not a thing. Streaming video, infinite scroll, and browser apps are definitely not a thing.

To access the internet, you have to use dial-up. This used your home telephone's landline connection. Why? Because in 1999, smartphones are not a thing.

The fastest speed on the information superhighway ran at about 56Kbs, about 0.01% the speed you're used to (400Mbps).

A single page could take 30 seconds to five minutes to load, depending on its content.

The Internet in the 1990s

  • Browsers: Netscape Navigator, Internet Explorer
  • Search Engines & Portals: Yahoo!, AltaVista, Lycos, Excite
  • Email & Messaging: AOL Mail, Hotmail, IRC, ICQ, AIM
  • Web Hosting & Personal Pages: Geocities, Angelfire, Tripod
  • Forums & Communities: Usenet, BBS boards, 2600 forums, early phpBB
  • E-Commerce & Auctions: eBay, Priceline, Amazon (mostly for books)
  • File Sharing & Downloads: Napster, FTP sites

How to connect to teh internet in 1999

  1. Buy a Dial-up Modem (if there isn't one already in your computer tower).
  2. Plug modem into the wall with a phone cord.
  3. Hope nobody needs to use the phone.
  4. Start-up modem by dialing the phone number to your ISP (via software like AOL or Netscape)
  5. Wait. . . and listen to this sound.
  6. w00t! Connected. Time to surf the web.
